Hi there!

You probably already know my name by now, but I'm Livianne! I'm born and raised in Los Angeles, CA and currently a 4th year student at UC San Diego.

My perspective on design started at home. Growing up and watching my older family members struggle with technology made me more aware of how inaccessible design can exclude people. It's what led me to focus on accessibility and information architecture. I design to help untangle this frustration, hoping to shape experiences that feel supportive.

Iteration

Learning by Doing

I started my journey in community college with nothing but a computer and a dream. At the time, I barely knew how to zoom in on Figma, but through curiosity, YouTube tutorials, and countless TikToks, I slowly taught myself design and prototyping from the ground up.

I still remember creating an outrageous looking burger and feeling like a creative genius. I didn't fully understand product design yet, I just knew I loved the building, learning, and improving aspects of it.

Exploration

Outside the File

When I'm not building projects, you'll usually find me cooking, baking, or experimenting in the kitchen! My love for food comes from the joy of bringing people together - something that also inspires the way I approach design and creativity. Whether through design, food, or music, I'm always exploring new ideas and turning them into meaningful, memorable experiences.
